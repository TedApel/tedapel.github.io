Clarinet bells are joined into a serpentine form, driven by a single horn speaker. The sound is produced by performing a negated sound analysis-synthesis of a passage from Messiaen's Abîme des Oiseaux, the solo clarinet movement from the Quartet for the End of Time. The sustained F# crescendo (section 2) is spectrally analyzed and negated — as the original note swells, the sculpture falls silent; as it recedes, the harmonics return. The result is a slowly evolving drone punctuated by absences, shaped by the acoustic pathway of the bells.

Named for Henri Akoka, the clarinetist who premiered the work in January 1941 at Stalag VIII-A, playing on a broken clarinet.
